## Card 1: Integrated Energy System Planning

*AI optimizing across electricity, heating, transport, and industry to find lowest-cost pathways to national targets*

### Case Study 1.1: United Kingdom - National Grid ESO Solar Nowcasting with Open Climate Fix

The United Kingdom's National Grid Electricity System Operator (ESO) partnered with Open Climate Fix, a nonprofit founded by former DeepMind researcher Jack Kelly, to deploy an AI-powered solar "nowcasting" service that has transformed how Britain's grid balances renewable energy. The system uses transformer-based machine learning models trained on satellite imagery to track cloud movements and predict solar generation from minutes to 36 hours ahead, updating forecasts every 15 minutes compared to the traditional six-hour intervals. Since full adoption, the Quartz Solar tool has halved forecasting errors compared to traditional weather-based methods, saving the grid operator an estimated GBP 30 million ($39 million) annually in reduced balancing costs by decreasing the reserve fossil fuel capacity needed as backup. The improved forecasting accuracy has enabled ESO to reduce reliance on idling gas plants, cutting both costs and carbon emissions while supporting Britain's transition to net zero. Open Climate Fix projects savings could increase to GBP 150 million annually by 2035 as the UK scales toward its government solar capacity targets.

**Source:** https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/ai-forecasts-solar-energy-uk/ and https://www.pv-magazine.com/2025/11/07/ai-powered-solar-forecasting-helps-uk-grid-operator-reduce-balancing-costs/

**Verification:** Savings figures reported by Open Climate Fix and verified through National Grid ESO's operational metrics tracking balancing costs before and after system deployment.

### Case Study 1.2: Denmark - Energinet AI-Enhanced Reserve Procurement

Denmark's national transmission system operator Energinet has deployed AI-enhanced weather forecasting to optimize its procurement of manual frequency restoration reserves (mFRR), fundamentally changing how the country balances its renewable-heavy grid. Before implementing the AI system, Energinet purchased a fixed day-ahead reserve of 900 MW daily across its two major grid zones; the new AI-based approach leverages machine learning to analyze weather patterns and forecast renewable generation variability, allowing dynamic adjustment of reserve purchases based on predicted conditions. During the test phase, the system successfully reduced the minimum reserve threshold to 800 MW while maintaining grid stability, with Denmark's grid already sourcing 54% of electricity from wind power - the highest share in the IEA. The initiative is part of Energinet's broader mandate to reach 100% renewable electricity by 2030, supported by their partnership with Microsoft and IBM to integrate AI across their operations including a generative AI solution using Azure OpenAI for legal and operational applications. Energinet expects the approach will enable them to triple electricity and gas transmission capacity without proportional infrastructure investments.

**Source:** https://www.irena.org/-/media/Files/IRENA/Agency/Publication/2025/Oct/IRENA_INN_Digitalisation_AI_for_power-systems_2025.pdf and https://pulse.microsoft.com/da-dk/making-a-difference-da-dk/manufacturing-da-dk/energinet-expects-to-triple-electricity-and-gas-capacity-with-ai/

**Verification:** Operational data from Energinet's test phase deployment, reported in IRENA's 2025 G7 report on Digitalization and AI for Power Systems.

### Case Study 1.3: Singapore - Energy Market Authority Solar PV Forecasting System (EMS II)

Singapore's Energy Market Authority (EMA) commissioned an enhanced Energy Management System (EMS II) in 2024 that integrates a machine learning-powered Solar Photovoltaic Forecasting Model to manage the city-state's transition to cleaner energy while maintaining one of the world's most reliable power grids. The system combines real-time data from island-wide solar irradiance sensors with satellite imagery and historical generation patterns, using machine learning algorithms to predict solar PV output and enable pre-emptive actions to manage solar intermittency. Singapore's proactive approach includes development of a Digital Network Twin that models the impact of growing electric vehicle demand on grid infrastructure under different scenarios, providing actionable insights for infrastructure upgrades. The EMA's two-year demand response sandbox launched in 2023 has already doubled registered capacity from 46 MW to 103 MW, while a 15 MW Virtual Power Plant pilot aggregating solar PV and battery storage is testing market participation. Singapore maintains exceptional grid reliability with an average of only 0.008 interruptions lasting 0.14 minutes per customer in 2023, demonstrating that advanced AI integration can enhance rather than compromise system stability.

**Source:** https://www.ema.gov.sg/news-events/news/media-releases/2024/future-proofing-ema-energy-management-system-for-a-sustainable-future and https://www.ema.gov.sg/news-events/news/media-releases/2023/leveraging-digital-solutions-to-future-proof-singapore-energy-grid

**Verification:** Official announcements from Singapore's Energy Market Authority with documented deployment timelines and performance metrics.

## Card 2: International Benchmarking

*AI processing datasets to compare national performance, identify best practices, adapt policies across borders*

### Case Study 2.1: European Union - ODYSSEE-MURE Energy Efficiency Scoreboard

The European Commission's ODYSSEE-MURE project, coordinated by ADEME with technical support from Enerdata and Fraunhofer, provides the most comprehensive data-driven benchmarking of energy efficiency performance across all EU Member States, Switzerland, and Energy Community countries. The system processes detailed energy consumption data, efficiency indicators, and CO2 emissions across residential, transport, industry, and services sectors using harmonized methodologies that enable meaningful cross-border comparisons and policy learning. The 2024 scoreboard assessed 28 countries using three criteria per nation - energy efficiency level reached, efficiency progress achieved, and policy impact scores - revealing that Luxembourg leads overall while Germany excels in policy pledges and no country excels across all sectors, signaling specific improvement opportunities. The database, containing data from 2000-2023 and covering nearly 40 national teams, enabled the identification that the EU's collective energy efficiency progress of only 1% in 2024 falls far short of the COP28 commitment to double the global average annual rate of efficiency improvements by 2030. The project's policy evaluation tools are now being enhanced for the 2025-2027 period to specifically support national implementation and monitoring of the Energy Efficiency Directive recast requirements.

**Source:** https://www.odyssee-mure.eu/publications/policy-brief/2024-scoreboard-european-energy-efficiency.html and https://www.odyssee-mure.eu/project.html

**Verification:** Scoreboard methodology and rankings published by ODYSSEE-MURE with transparent indicator definitions and annual updates validated by national statistical agencies.

### Case Study 2.2: International Energy Agency - Energy and AI Observatory with Global Benchmarking

The International Energy Agency launched its Energy and AI Observatory in 2024-2025 as part of a major "Energy for AI, and AI for Energy" initiative, creating the most authoritative global platform for comparing how nations are deploying AI across their energy sectors. The Observatory features interactive tools that track data centre electricity consumption by region, revealing that the United States accounts for 45% of global data centre energy use, followed by China at 25% and Europe at 15%, with consumption growing 12% annually since 2017 - four times faster than total electricity demand. Twenty detailed case studies document how AI is being deployed across the energy sector in different countries, from schools in Stockholm using AI to optimize heating systems to steel plants in Czechia and Spain leveraging machine learning to reduce gas consumption. The IEA's April 2025 "Energy and AI" report provides the most comprehensive data-driven global analysis of the energy-AI nexus, enabling countries to benchmark their approaches against international best practices and identify policy gaps. The Observatory directly supports the Global Conference on Energy and AI that the IEA organized, bringing together policymakers from member states to share cross-border lessons and accelerate technology transfer.

**Source:** https://www.iea.org/data-and-statistics/data-tools/energy-and-ai-observatory and https://www.iea.org/reports/energy-and-ai

**Verification:** Published IEA datasets with documented methodologies and sources, representing the agency's flagship data product for member state comparisons.

### Case Study 2.3: Climate TRACE - Independent AI-Powered Global Emissions Inventory

Climate TRACE, co-led by former U.S. Vice President Al Gore and a consortium including WattTime, provides the first independent global emissions tracking platform that does not rely on self-reported national data, enabling objective cross-country comparisons and identification of reporting gaps. The coalition harnesses AI and machine learning to analyze over 59 trillion bytes of data from more than 300 satellites, 11,100 sensors, and multiple additional sources to track emissions from 745 million individual assets worldwide - a 4,400-fold increase in granularity from previous versions when the December 2023 release covered 352 million assets. The platform's independent analysis revealed that actual emissions from global oil and gas production were approximately double what had been self-reported to the United Nations, with the top 14 biggest emitters all being oil and gas fields, providing critical benchmarking data for identifying where national inventories need improvement. Climate TRACE and The Climate Group launched the States and Regions Remote Sensing (STARRS) project to provide emissions inventories specifically for sub-national governments, enabling regional benchmarking against national and international peers. The data is now used to support both national-level policy assessments and facility-specific accountability, with emissions estimates spanning 2015-2024 annually and monthly breakdowns from 2021 onward.

**Source:** https://climatetrace.org/ and https://www.theclimategroup.org/our-work/press/states-and-regions-have-new-method-cut-their-emissions-using-satellites-remote

**Verification:** Independent validation through comparison with existing inventories; note that a 2024 analysis found Climate TRACE underestimates some power plant emissions by ~50% versus EPA data, indicating ongoing calibration work.

## Card 3: Climate Commitment Tracking

*AI monitoring national progress toward Paris Agreement commitments, flagging countries falling behind*

### Case Study 3.1: UNFCCC-Microsoft AI Partnership for Paris Agreement Tracking

At COP28 in November 2023, the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change and Microsoft announced a $3 million partnership to build an AI-powered platform and global climate data hub that will transform how the 196 Paris Agreement signatories measure and report carbon emissions. The platform will enable automated analysis and validation of climate data from transportation, agriculture, industrial processes, and other sectors, replacing manual and time-consuming carbon data aggregation processes with digital automation and advanced analytics. The system will create Global Climate Dashboards for the UNFCCC website providing real-time transparency into national progress, with planning tools using simulations, benchmarks, and data visualizations to help countries understand their trajectories. As Microsoft President Brad Smith stated at the announcement, "you can't fix what you can't measure, and these new AI and data tools will allow nations to measure emissions far better than they can today," directly supporting implementation of the Enhanced Transparency Framework and Global Stocktake mechanisms. The initiative aims to save countries time and money while enabling targeted climate action and accountability toward the Paris Agreement goal of limiting warming to 1.5 degrees Celsius.

**Source:** https://news.microsoft.com/source/2023/11/30/unfccc-partners-with-microsoft-to-use-ai-and-advanced-data-technology-to-track-global-carbon-emissions-and-assess-progress-under-the-paris-agreement/

**Verification:** Official joint announcement by UNFCCC and Microsoft with committed funding amounts and specific platform capabilities detailed.

### Case Study 3.2: Climate Action Tracker - AI-Enhanced NDC Assessment Methodology

The Climate Action Tracker (CAT), an independent scientific project produced by Climate Analytics and NewClimate Institute, tracks government climate action across 32 countries covering approximately 80% of global emissions and rates their progress against Paris Agreement targets using increasingly sophisticated data analysis methods. The CAT rates NDC targets against each country's fair share contribution considering responsibility, capability, and equality principles, while also comparing against indicative national emissions from global least-cost pathways derived from the IPCC's Sixth Assessment Report models. Their 2024 assessment revealed critical gaps, including downgrading the EU's policies and action rating from "Almost sufficient" to "Insufficient" due to updated modeling, and projecting that China, India, Japan, Canada, South Korea, and Indonesia will miss their NDC commitments by significant margins while Russia is on track to exceed targets. A complementary Nature Communications study demonstrated how AI-based machine learning classifiers and natural language processing can evaluate alignment between NDCs and Voluntary National Review reports across 67 countries, finding that middle- and low-income high-emission countries tend to have low NDC targets with inconsistent national planning documents. The CAT's 2035 Climate Target Update Tracker is now monitoring submission of new NDCs ahead of the February 2025 deadline, providing real-time accountability for the next round of climate commitments.

**Source:** https://climateactiontracker.org/ and https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-024-53956-1

**Verification:** Methodology published in peer-reviewed journals and technical documentation; assessment updates released with each major COP and synchronized with IPCC report cycles.

### Case Study 3.3: World Resources Institute - Climate Watch NDC Tracking Platform

The World Resources Institute's Climate Watch platform provides the most comprehensive open-data system for tracking how countries are implementing their Paris Agreement commitments, analyzing and comparing Nationally Determined Contributions using over 150 structured indicators. The platform tracks global and country-level GHG emissions alongside climate commitments, enabling researchers and policymakers to search NDC content for key terms and quantify the impact of target changes on projected emissions trajectories. WRI's NDC Tracker monitors new submissions in real-time, with the platform currently tracking over 100 countries representing more than 70% of global emissions that have submitted new NDCs for the 2025 cycle. The system enables assessment of whether submissions reflect COP28 Global Stocktake outcomes, which called for shifts away from fossil fuels, scaling renewable energy, and deploying zero-emission vehicles. WRI hosted a December 2024 webinar providing live demonstrations of the tracker's capabilities as governments prepared submissions ahead of the February 2025 deadline, making this a primary accountability tool for civil society, researchers, and governments monitoring Paris Agreement implementation progress.

**Source:** https://www.wri.org/initiatives/climate-watch and https://www.wri.org/ndcs/tracking-progress

**Verification:** Open data platform with transparent methodology documentation; used by NDC Partnership and multiple UN bodies as a reference source.

## Additional Notable Case Studies (Supplementary)

### China - National AI-Energy Integration Strategy (2024-2030)

China's National Energy Administration released a comprehensive plan in 2024 to integrate artificial intelligence across its entire energy sector, targeting development of five sector-specific large language models for grid, power generation, coal mining, and oil/gas by 2027, with 10+ replicable "AI+energy" pilot projects at national scale. By 2030, China aims to establish world-leading energy-specific AI capabilities including breakthroughs in smart grid dispatch, energy resource exploration, and renewable generation forecasting. The strategy directly addresses the challenge of integrating vast amounts of intermittent renewable energy into a grid that must balance supply and demand second by second.

**Source:** https://english.www.gov.cn/news/202509/08/content_WS68be8c3ec6d0868f4e8f566d.html

---

### United States - DOE AI for Energy Initiative and Grid Modernization

Following President Biden's Executive Order 14110 on AI (October 2023), the U.S. Department of Energy released comprehensive reports on AI for grid modernization and launched the VoltAIc Initiative with $13 million in funding to develop PolicyAI for streamlining infrastructure permitting. Pacific Northwest National Laboratory's PermitAI prototype has created an AI-searchable database of 2,917 environmental impact statements (3.6 million tokens), while the NREL SolarAPP+ tool had saved over 33,000 hours of permitting staff time by end of 2023. The DOE estimates nearly half of identified AI applications are high-impact and ready for immediate deployment.

**Source:** https://www.energy.gov/sites/default/files/2024-04/AI%20EO%20Report%20Section%205.2g(i)_043024.pdf and https://www.pnnl.gov/publications/ai-energy-report-features-pnnl-expertise

---

### Australia - AEMO Integrated System Plan with AI-Enhanced Forecasting

The Australian Energy Market Operator's 2024 Integrated System Plan provides a 30-year AI-informed roadmap for transitioning the National Electricity Market to net zero, calling for AU$16 billion in investment and 10,000km of new transmission. AEMO now manages grid conditions where wind and solar account for over 70% of supply at times, using the Australian Wind Energy Forecasting System and partnerships with the Bureau of Meteorology for weather-based renewable prediction. The operator is targeting 100% instantaneous renewable penetration capability on any day.

**Source:** https://www.aemo.com.au/energy-systems/major-publications/integrated-system-plan-isp/2024-integrated-system-plan-isp
