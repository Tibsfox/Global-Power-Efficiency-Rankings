# Community-Level AI Energy: New Card Research

**Research Date:** 2026-01-28
**Domain:** Additional community-level AI applications for energy efficiency
**Overall Confidence:** MEDIUM-HIGH

## Research Summary

This research identifies two new high-impact AI application areas for community-level energy efficiency that complement the existing three cards (Grant Writing, Community Organizing, Data Analysis for Advocacy). The recommended new cards cover:

1. **AI-Powered Building Energy Benchmarking** - Helping municipalities and community organizations track, analyze, and improve building energy performance at scale
2. **AI for Energy Burden Identification & Outreach** - Using machine learning to identify energy-burdened households and target assistance programs more effectively

Both areas have strong, documented real-world implementations with measurable outcomes and are directly actionable for community groups, nonprofits, and local governments.

---

## Recommended Card 4: AI-Powered Building Energy Benchmarking

### Why This Deserves a Card

Building energy benchmarking is now mandated in 40+ U.S. cities and states, affecting thousands of community buildings including municipal facilities, schools, affordable housing, and nonprofit properties. AI and automation tools are transforming how communities:
- Track energy performance across building portfolios
- Identify buildings that need retrofits
- Demonstrate compliance with building performance standards
- Make data-driven decisions about energy investments

This is distinct from the existing cards because it focuses on **building-level tracking and compliance** rather than grant writing, organizing, or advocacy. Community organizations are increasingly required to benchmark their buildings and can use AI tools to simplify compliance while identifying savings opportunities.

### AI Capability Demonstrated

- Automated data ingestion from utility meters and ENERGY STAR Portfolio Manager
- Machine learning for anomaly detection and building performance analysis
- Predictive analytics for retrofit prioritization
- Automated compliance tracking and reporting

### Case Study 4.1: Touchstone IQ - Municipal Energy Benchmarking Across North America (2014-2025)

Touchstone IQ has emerged as the leading implementer of building energy benchmarking and building performance laws in North America, providing AI-enhanced software that aggregates complex energy data and automates analytics, benchmarking, and forecasting. Founded in 2014 by energy efficiency engineers and software developers, the platform now serves twenty governments across the U.S. and Canada managing energy benchmarking and building performance programs. In 2025, cities using Touchstone's platform continued to prioritize high compliance because benchmarking and building performance standards only drive emissions reductions when reporting is complete, accurate, and verified. The system uses automated workflows, built-in data quality checks, and clear progress tracking to reduce administrative burden for program teams. Notable 2025 milestones include: Miami expanded to commercial buildings, more than doubling program participation; Minnesota launched its state-wide energy benchmarking program with Touchstone's software hub; and Seattle partnered with Touchstone on software enhancements debuting in summer 2026 for jurisdictions using the ENERGY STAR Portfolio Manager web services API.

**Measurable outcomes:**
- 20+ government jurisdictions served
- Built-in quality controls and automated compliance status notifications
- Expansion to multiple new cities in 2025 (Miami, Minnesota state program)

**Source:** [Touchstone IQ 2025 Impact Report](https://touchstoneiq.com/governments/our-clients/2025-impact-report) | [Touchstone IQ Government Software](https://touchstoneiq.com/government-software)

### Case Study 4.2: NEEP BEAM Tool - Building Energy Analysis for Boston, Pittsburgh, Denver, and Reno (2023-2025)

The Building Energy Analysis Manager (BEAM) tool, developed by Northeast Energy Efficiency Partnerships (NEEP), is a cost-effective solution designed to help towns, cities, and states manage their building energy policy goals. BEAM expands upon the U.S. Department of Energy's SEED Platform by adding complex automatic compliance tracking based on each jurisdiction's specific building performance standard or benchmarking policy. The tool's flexible structure accommodates various policy designs including building type, building size, compliance timelines, and different units of measure (ENERGY STAR Score, EUI, GHG emissions, or jurisdiction-specific scores). In a phase one pilot project, Boston, Pittsburgh, Denver, and Reno are using BEAM to support the implementation of benchmarking and building performance standards in their cities. Across the NEEP region, eight states, at least eight municipalities, and one county have now passed benchmarking ordinances, with New York City and Washington D.C. serving as early adopters who are now putting benchmarking data into action through building energy performance standards (BEPS).

**Measurable outcomes:**
- 4 major cities in pilot program (Boston, Pittsburgh, Denver, Reno)
- 8 states + 8 municipalities in NEEP region with benchmarking ordinances
- Automatic compliance tracking across multiple policy frameworks

**Source:** [NEEP - BEAM Building Energy Analysis Manager](https://neep.org/solutions-low-carbon-states-and-communities/beam-building-energy-analysis-manager) | [NEEP Benchmarking Toolkit](https://neep.org/energy-rating/benchmarking-toolkit)

### Case Study 4.3: BlocPower AI Building Assessment - 125 Million U.S. Buildings Analyzed (2023-2024)

BlocPower, a Brooklyn-based climate technology company, assembled a dataset covering every building in the United States and created a digital model of all 125 million buildings. The team ran these models through an open source thermodynamic energy program from the Department of Energy to develop a generative AI model that provides customized decarbonization recommendations for taking any building in America to net zero. This free tool offers personalized retrofit plans for each building based on its specific characteristics. BlocPower has raised more than $250 million in total funding, including nearly $155 million in 2023 from investors including Microsoft Climate Innovation Fund, Goldman Sachs, New York State Ventures, and NBA player Russell Westbrook. The company partnered with the City of Ithaca, New York in 2022 to retrofit its entire building stock by 2030 - the first city-wide electrification initiative of its kind in the United States. They have since launched decarbonization efforts in Denver, Oakland, San Jose, and Menlo Park. Additionally, BlocPower secured a $108 million contract with New York City to train up to 3,000 workers annually at new training facilities in Brooklyn and the Bronx, focusing on communities with high unemployment.

**Measurable outcomes:**
- 125 million buildings analyzed with AI-powered decarbonization recommendations
- $250+ million in funding raised
- Ithaca, NY: first U.S. city committed to retrofit entire building stock by 2030
- $108 million NYC contract, 3,000 workers trained annually

**Source:** [CNBC - Microsoft, Kimbal Musk invest in BlocPower](https://www.cnbc.com/2023/03/02/microsoft-kimbal-musk-invest-in-climate-tech-company-blocpower.html) | [BlocPower Race to Decarbonize](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20240725040930/en/BlocPower-Announces-The-Race-to-Decarbonize)

**Verification Notes:** In 2025, BlocPower faced challenges including CEO resignation and some abandoned projects, which should be noted when presenting this as a case study. However, the AI building analysis tool and city partnerships remain documented achievements.

## Recommended Card 5: AI for Energy Burden Identification & Outreach

### Why This Deserves a Card

Approximately 31% of U.S. households experience energy insecurity, yet many eligible households don't know about or aren't enrolled in assistance programs. AI and machine learning are now being used to:
- Predict which households are likely energy-burdened using utility and demographic data
- Identify eligible households not yet enrolled in assistance programs
- Target outreach more effectively to reach underserved communities
- Personalize communications to increase program enrollment

This is distinct from the existing "Data Analysis for Advocacy" card because it focuses on **proactive identification and enrollment** of energy-burdened households rather than advocacy work. It directly addresses the "last mile" problem of connecting eligible households with existing programs.

### AI Capability Demonstrated

- Machine learning models for income and energy burden prediction
- Predictive analytics using utility meter data and demographic information
- AI-powered customer segmentation for targeted outreach
- Personalized communication generation based on household characteristics

### Case Study 5.1: Oracle Opower - AI-Powered Energy Burden Prediction and Low-Income Outreach (2009-2025)

Oracle's Opower platform, used by more than 175 utilities worldwide, combines AI with behavioral science to identify financially vulnerable customers and predict their program eligibility for energy assistance. The Opower data science team uses deep learning to predict energy burden at a personal, household level, addressing a critical challenge: while low-income households use less energy per capita, they spend a considerably larger portion of income on energy bills. Oracle surveys found that 86% of likely low-income utility customers worried about affording their energy bills, with 64% having received a shutoff notice within the past year. Opower's limited-income solutions apply predictive analytics to utility, third-party, and customer data to proactively reach customers with program offers, help prevent collections or disconnections, and tailor service outreach with home energy reports and high-bill alerts. In December 2025, Xcel Energy (serving 3.9 million electric customers) selected Opower to provide comparative home usage reports, behavioral demand-response messages, and AI-driven real-time analytics. Since 2009, Opower programs have saved customers $3.6 billion and conserved 38 terawatt-hours of energy - equivalent to powering 3.8 million homes annually. PG&E's Opower program alone has delivered over 1.5 terawatt-hours of electric savings and $542 million in customer bill savings.

**Measurable outcomes:**
- 175+ utilities using the platform
- 38 TWh of energy saved since 2009
- $3.6 billion in customer savings
- 86% of likely low-income customers identified as worried about energy bills
- 1.5 TWh electric savings and $542 million bill savings for PG&E customers alone

**Source:** [Oracle Opower Energy Efficiency](https://www.oracle.com/utilities/opower-energy-efficiency/) | [Oracle - Using AI for Predicting Personalized Energy Burden](https://www.oracle.com/utilities/using-ai-for-predicting-personalized-energy-burden-and-gas-bill/)

### Case Study 5.2: National Grid LEAD Tool Integration - Identifying 45,000 Additional Eligible Households (2019-2023)

National Grid, serving Long Island, New York, demonstrated how combining the DOE's Low-Income Energy Affordability Data (LEAD) Tool with internal utility data can identify underserved households eligible for energy affordability programs. National Grid used the LEAD Tool to support implementation of its Home Energy Affordability Team (HEAT) weatherization program for income-eligible residential natural gas customers. By analyzing LEAD Tool data at the census tract level, National Grid validated their internal data on low-income customers and - critically - identified an additional 45,000 households in their service area who were eligible for energy assistance programs but not yet enrolled. This analysis directly informed expanded marketing efforts to reach these previously unidentified eligible households with information about weatherization services and bill assistance. The case demonstrates how AI-enhanced energy burden data tools can close the gap between program eligibility and actual enrollment, ensuring that energy affordability assistance reaches households who qualify but may not know about available programs.

**Measurable outcomes:**
- 45,000 additional eligible households identified
- Direct integration with HEAT weatherization program marketing
- Census tract-level analysis for targeted outreach

**Source:** [DOE - National Grid: Using the LEAD Tool to Target Energy Affordability Services](https://www.energy.gov/scep/slsc/articles/national-grid-using-lead-tool-target-energy-affordability-services-eligible) | [Census Bureau - LEAD Tool Data Story](https://www.census.gov/programs-surveys/acs/about/acs-data-stories/lead-tool.html)

### Case Study 5.3: Recurve Analytics - Meter-Based Targeting for Low-Income Heat Pump Programs (2024-2025)

Recurve Analytics, developer of the DOE-approved OpenEEmeter software, worked with Southern California Edison (SCE) to apply machine learning and smart meter data analysis to identify low-income customers who would benefit most from heat pump retrofits under the Energy Savings Assistance (ESA) program. By applying demographic filters and analyzing energy usage patterns, Recurve identified customers for whom electrification would likely result in significant bill savings - critical because program success depends on finding participants with the highest opportunity to see their energy bills decrease. The targeted approach resulted in nearly 18,000 personalized emails sent to eligible low-income customers promoting ESA heat pump retrofit programs. This initiative establishes a potentially industry-changing best practice: using smart meter data and analytics to ensure electrification translates into tangible savings for customers. In January 2025, the U.S. Department of Energy named Recurve's OpenEEmeter as the first - and currently only - approved solution for measured pathway software verification in the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) Home Efficiency Rebates (HOMES) Program, validating the approach for measuring actual energy savings from retrofits.

**Measurable outcomes:**
- 18,000 targeted emails to eligible low-income customers
- First DOE-approved software for IRA HOMES Program measured savings
- Integration with SCE Energy Savings Assistance program
- Focus on "do no harm" - ensuring electrification doesn't increase energy burden

**Source:** [Recurve - Energy Solutions Boost Savings for Low-Income Heat Pump Customers](https://www.recurve.com/blog/energy-solutions-recurve-boost-savings-for-low-income-heat-pump-customers-with-meter-based-insights) | [Recurve OpenEEmeter DOE Approval](https://recurve.com/2025/01/17/recurves-openeemeter-approved-by-doe-as-first-open-source-mv-software-for-ira-home-energy-rebates/)

## Supplemental Research: Additional AI Applications Considered

### Google Project Sunroof / Environmental Insights Explorer

Google's Project Sunroof uses machine learning and 3D modeling from Google Earth to calculate solar potential for every building in its coverage area. The Data Explorer tool enables solar estimates for entire communities, helping cities and municipalities visualize how many rooftops are suitable for solar and how much carbon could be displaced. In Fresno, California, 96% of rooftops are viable for solar; if all reached capacity, the carbon offset would equal taking 278,000 cars off the road annually. Houston has the highest solar potential of any U.S. city at 18,940 GWh per year.

**Why not selected as a card:** The existing "Data Analysis for Energy Advocacy" card already covers data tools for energy planning. Project Sunroof data is also noted to be outdated in some areas (last updated 2018 for some regions), limiting its immediate actionability.

**Source:** [Google Project Sunroof](https://sunroof.withgoogle.com/) | [Google - Shedding Light on Solar Potential](https://blog.google/products/maps/shedding-light-solar-potential-all-50-us-states/)

### IBM Open Building Insights - Kenya Energy Planning

IBM, in partnership with Sustainable Energy for All, launched Open Building Insights (OBI) at COP29 - an AI-powered platform that provides building location, height, footprint area, and usage type information for energy planning. In Kenya, Makueni County used OBI insights projected to benefit 1,139,000 citizens by 2030. The platform combines AI models from IBM (built on watsonx), German Aerospace Center height estimates, and Open Energy Maps electricity data.

**Why not selected as a card:** This is primarily an international development tool rather than U.S. community-focused, and overlaps with the data analysis card concept.

**Source:** [IBM - Sustainable Energy for All and IBM Launch AI Solutions](https://newsroom.ibm.com/2024-11-14-sustainable-energy-for-all-and-ibm-launch-new-ai-solutions-for-energy-and-urban-development)

### AI Community Microgrid Management

Research shows AI-optimized microgrids can achieve up to 98.23% carbon emission reduction and 65.45% cost reduction compared to traditional grid systems. The Brooklyn Microgrid (LO3 Energy) demonstrated peer-to-peer energy trading with 6-12% customer savings and 18-37% increased revenue for energy producers. LO3 was later acquired by Shell.

**Why not selected as a card:** While technically sophisticated, community microgrids remain rare and face significant regulatory barriers. Most community organizations cannot implement this in the near term, making it less immediately actionable than benchmarking or outreach targeting.

**Source:** [Brooklyn Microgrid](https://www.brooklyn.energy/) | [ScienceDirect - AI-based community microgrids](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0306261924019068)
