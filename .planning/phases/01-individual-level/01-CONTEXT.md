<decisions>
## Implementation Decisions

### Card Layout Structure
- All content inline (scrollable) — no tabs or accordions
- Integrated flow — new sections weave into existing card structure rather than appearing as separate blocks
- Long cards okay — full content visible without truncation

### Case Study Presentation
- Full detail always visible — organization name, what they did, measurable outcome, source
- Footnote-style citations — small superscript numbers linking to sources at bottom of section
- Factual/journalistic tone — "Sense energy monitors deployed in 500 Sacramento homes achieved 15% reduction"

### Career Suggestions Format
- Full transition paths — current background → role, required skills, salary range (if reliable), resource links
- Diverse organization — each card's 5 paths show variety across starting backgrounds, target roles, and transition timelines
- Free resources priority — emphasize free courses, certifications, and open materials

### Project Ideas Format
- DIY and Commercial clearly separated — distinct subsections within project ideas

### Visual Hierarchy
- Equal visual weight — all three content types (case studies, projects, careers) have similar prominence
- Complementary variation — new sections can have slightly different treatment while staying cohesive with existing design

### Claude's Discretion
- Section separators — choose what looks clean with existing design (clear breaks vs subtle transitions)
- Outcome emphasis — decide whether to bold metrics, use callout boxes, or keep plain text
- Visual differentiation — whether to use color-coding, icons, or headers-only for content types
- Salary inclusion — include ranges if data is reliable, omit if speculative

</decisions>
