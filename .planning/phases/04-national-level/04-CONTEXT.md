## Implementation Decisions

### Visual Identity
- Color accent: Claude's discretion (distinct from green/blue/cyan used in previous levels)

### Case Study Focus
- Organizational mix: Balanced between federal agencies (DOE, EIA, EPA, FERC) AND large utilities (TVA, BPA, large IOUs)
- International comparisons: Yes — include benchmarks showing how other countries (UK, Germany, Japan) handle similar national-scale challenges
- Distinction from State/Regional: Both scale/coordination aspects (cross-state coordination, national grid interconnections, federal policy implementation) AND policy-setting aspects (standards, regulations)
- Recency: Mix of recent initiatives (2022-2025, IRA implementation, DOE AI initiatives) AND established programs with proven track records

### Career Transitions
- Destination balance: Representation across federal agencies, national labs (NREL, Argonne, PNNL), and large utilities
- Oil/gas transition: No special emphasis — treat same as any other industry origin
- Origin backgrounds: Diverse mix including general professionals (data analysts, project managers), adjacent energy sectors (state commissions, regional ISOs), and tech sector crossovers (software engineers, ML specialists)
- Security clearances: Do not address — keep focus on skills and qualifications

### DIY Project Scope
- Target audience: Both individual learners AND state agency staff — projects that work for skill-building AND have practical state-level applications
- Data sources: Claude's discretion — select appropriate federal, academic, or nonprofit sources per project
- Analysis scope: Mix of cross-state comparison analyses AND national aggregate analyses
- Tool complexity: **Accessible to domain experts without coding skills** — use AI tools to leverage lifetime of expertise, spark imagination for career changers, demonstrate how AI assists learning through real-world problems

### Claude's Discretion
- Color accent selection (must be distinct from green, blue, cyan)
- Additional visual treatments if warranted
- Case study border override approach
- Data source selection per DIY project
- Technical grid terminology explanations (inline on first use, following State/Regional pattern)
