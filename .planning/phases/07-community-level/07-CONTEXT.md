# Phase 7: Community Level - Context

**Gathered:** 2026-01-29
**Status:** Ready for planning

<domain>
## Phase Boundary

Add personalized learning paths with AI prompts and self-study resources to all 25 Community-level career transition pathways. Focus on mission-driven, collaborative impact with connector learning styles. Prompts and patterns must differ from Phase 6's hands-on Individual approach.

</domain>

<decisions>
## Implementation Decisions

### Collaboration Framing
- Mix individual contributor and team facilitator framing by pathway — some roles suit "volunteer your skills to help an org," others suit "coordinate a working group"
- Project deliverables scale by proficiency: Beginner = presentation-ready artifacts (reports, proposals), Proficient = working implementations (dashboards, tools)
- Light touch on soft skills — mention collaboration context briefly without dedicated skill-building content

### Mission-Driven Tone
- Skills first, impact woven in — lead with what you'll learn, connect to community benefit along the way
- Define nonprofit terminology when used — terms like "capacity building" or "stakeholder engagement" get brief contextual explanation
- Explicitly include equity framing — prompts mention "serving underserved communities" and access language where relevant
- Connector learning style via both networking emphasis (associations, peer groups) AND relationship-based project framing

### Resource Accessibility
- Low-cost resources acceptable (up to ~$50 if high value), not strictly free-only
- AI as accelerator approach — external resources as foundation, AI prompts to customize and extend them
- Both tool-building prompts (create spreadsheets, templates, dashboards) AND analytical prompts (analyze situations, recommend approaches)
- Show iterative AI use — include follow-up prompt examples ("Now ask Claude to refine based on...")

### Prompt Pattern Variety
- Voice varies by role type: technical roles get advisor voice ("Help me advise..."), coordination roles get facilitator voice ("As someone coordinating...")
- Occasionally use stakeholder personas to add variety ("A food bank director asks you...")
- Opening hooks vary across pathways — mix of impact verbs, collaborative verbs, and other styles to avoid repetition
- Show multi-step AI workflows — demonstrate chaining prompts for complex outcomes

### Claude's Discretion
- Specificity of org type references (food bank vs. "local nonprofit") per pathway context
- Where scoping prompts fit naturally for volunteer/pro-bono pathways
- Exact balance of workflow examples across the 5 Community sections

</decisions>

- Emphasize how users can use AI tools to build their own solutions and skill sets — empowerment through AI, not just consuming pre-packaged training
- Show how AI can take existing knowledge/resources and help users adapt them to their specific community context

</specifics>
